This repository was created to document my journey and evolution in competitive programming, specifically through problems solved on Codeforces. After spending some time practicing on LeetCode, I felt the need to explore new platforms and face different styles of challenges to keep learning and pushing my limits.

All solutions are stored directly in the root of this project, making it a straightforward archive of my progress. Each file represents a challenge overcome and a step forward in my learning process.

The main reasons I am dedicated to this repository are:

* **Developing Better Problem-Solving Intuition:** Codeforces problems often require creative thinking and mathematical insights before even writing the first line of code. I want to train my brain to spot these patterns faster.
* **Learning Advanced Concepts:** I want to gradually study and implement more complex algorithms and data structures that are highly demanded in competitive programming, such as advanced graph theory, dynamic programming, and number theory.
* **The Discipline of Upsolving:** One of my major focuses is working on the questions I failed to solve during active rounds. I believe that true growth happens when I sit down to debug and finally solve a problem that originally defeated me during the contest time limit.
* **Consistency:** Keeping this repository active serves as a personal journal of my dedication, helping me stay consistent even when the problems get tough.

This is a continuous work in progress, driven by my interest in discovering different ways to think about data structures and tackling complex problems.
