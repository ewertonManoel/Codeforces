This repository was created to document my journey and evolution in competitive programming through problems solved on Codeforces. It serves as a record of my progress, where I continuously challenge myself with increasingly difficult problems while developing stronger algorithmic thinking and problem-solving skills.

All solutions are stored directly in the root of this project, making it a straightforward archive of my progress. Each file represents a challenge overcome and another step forward in my learning process.

The main reasons I am dedicated to this repository are:

* **Developing Better Problem-Solving Intuition:** Codeforces problems often require creative thinking and mathematical insights before even writing the first line of code. I want to train my brain to recognize these patterns more naturally.
* **Learning Advanced Concepts:** I want to gradually study and implement more complex algorithms and data structures that are highly demanded in competitive programming, such as advanced graph theory, dynamic programming, and number theory.
* **The Discipline of Upsolving:** One of my major focuses is revisiting the problems I could not solve during contests. I believe that true growth comes from understanding my mistakes and eventually finding the correct solution.
* **Improving Under Pressure:** Participating in contests helps me develop the ability to analyze problems quickly, make decisions efficiently, and write correct solutions within strict time limits.
* **Consistency:** Keeping this repository active serves as a personal journal of my dedication, helping me stay consistent even when the problems become more challenging.

This is a continuous work in progress, driven by my interest in discovering different ways to think about algorithms, data structures, and competitive programming.
